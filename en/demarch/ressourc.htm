Resources and Societies

 

Research on environment touches on natural resource and externality issues. It deals with the management of renewable resources, the management of production externalities (i.e. pollution) and the management of multiple-use ecosystems. In all cases, the issue is to define and implement rules of collective use.

Both for the resources and the externalities, analysis must take into account natural and social dynamics.

The natural dynamics are characterized by the interaction of multiple processes involving different species and resources, at different spatial and temporal scales. · The social processes involve different actors and several organizational levels ranging from individuals and user communities to the large institutions that are responsible for development.
