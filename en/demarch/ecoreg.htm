Ecoregionality

 

In these last few years, a debate has begun in the agronomic domain to improve the approach to problems of managing natural resources. The attempt is to reconcile two approaches. Studies at the level of agricultural exploitation did not take into account changes that took place at larger scales. And, inversely, the models of economic forecasting did not take into account the diversity of the processes at smaller scales. The choice of the ecoregion brings agriculturists and economists to work at the same scale to confront natural and social dynamics (Rabbinge, 1995).

The ecoregional approach is applied successively to the nature of regional space, the components of the regional analysis (seen from the viewpoint of the system, space, or policies), and the use of a regional model (Manichon, 1997). Once the scale is thus defined, the question of scale transfer and integration remains. In fact, the integration of the dynamics on a space consisting of multiple resources and actors with diverse behaviors cannot be reduced to a simple exercise of nesting of Russian dolls. The image of interleaved hierarchical dynamics seems more appropriate.

References

Manichon H., 1997. L'écorégionalité dans la recherche pour le développement. Propositions de la recherche française. Cirad, Montpellier, France.
